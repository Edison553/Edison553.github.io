Our Research

Our lab focuses on understanding the neural mechanisms of schizophrenia through advanced fMRI analysis, computational modeling, and integration with genetic data.

Research Directions

Functional Connectivity Mapping

We use resting-state and task-based fMRI to map abnormal functional connectivity patterns in schizophrenia. Our work focuses on identifying disrupted brain networks that underlie cognitive and behavioral symptoms.

  • Application of graph theory to analyze network topology
  • Investigation of default mode network dysregulation
  • Use of Granger causality to infer directional connectivity

Genetic-Neuroimaging Integration

By combining fMRI data with genetic information from GWAS and SNP studies, we aim to identify how specific genetic variants influence brain function in schizophrenia.

  • Multi-modal analysis of imaging and genetic datasets
  • Identification of gene-expression patterns in brain regions
  • Exploration of polygenic risk scores and brain connectivity

Machine Learning and Biomarker Discovery

We apply advanced machine learning techniques to identify novel biomarkers for schizophrenia diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment response prediction.

  • Development of predictive models using neuroimaging features
  • Integration of clinical, imaging, and genetic data for personalized medicine
  • Validation of biomarkers in independent cohorts

Translational Neuroscience

Our translational research aims to bridge basic science discoveries with clinical applications, including the development of novel therapeutic strategies.

  • Investigation of brain mechanisms underlying treatment response
  • Development of fMRI-based biomarkers for treatment optimization
  • Collaboration with clinical teams to test novel interventions

Research Methods

fMRI Acquisition & Analysis

Advanced fMRI protocols including resting-state, task-based, and diffusion tensor imaging (DTI).

Mathematical Modeling

Application of graph theory, Granger causality, and dynamic causal modeling to fMRI data.

Big Data Analytics

Handling and integration of large-scale neuroimaging and genetic datasets using high-performance computing.

Machine Learning

Supervised and unsupervised learning techniques for pattern recognition and biomarker discovery.

Genetic Analysis

Integration of GWAS, SNP, and gene expression data with neuroimaging phenotypes.

Computational Modeling

Development of computational models to simulate brain activity and connectivity patterns.
